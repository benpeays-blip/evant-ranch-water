# Evant Ranch Water Collection Project Brief

We are building a project-management website for a ranch-scale water collection and water resilience project.

The ranch is approximately 500 acres near Evant, Texas. There is a ranch house believed to be roughly 3,000 square feet with gutters. The initial plan is to collect roof rainwater, run it about 100 yards to a large storage tank, store it there, and pump it back uphill or to points of use when needed.

The ranch currently has a well, likely deep, but the well depth, aquifer, pump depth, static water level, pumping level, gallons per minute, and water quality still need to be confirmed.

This project should not be treated as just installing a tank. It should be treated as a whole ranch water resilience plan with roof rainwater collection, well backup, tank sizing, pumping, water treatment, contractor task management, and ranch-scale water planning.

Important assumptions:
- Ranch: approximately 500 acres
- Location: near Evant, Texas
- Roof catchment estimate: approximately 3,000 sq. ft.
- Distance from house to tank: approximately 100 yards / 300 feet
- Average annual rainfall estimate: approximately 30.7 inches
- Rainwater formula: rainfall inches × 0.623 × catchment square feet × runoff efficiency
- 1 gallon of water weighs approximately 8.34 pounds
- 2.31 feet of vertical lift equals approximately 1 psi

Key warnings:
- Storage does not create water; roof area and rainfall create water.
- A 3,000 sq. ft. roof is useful but not huge for a 500-acre ranch.
- A 30,000–50,000 gallon tank may be realistic for the current house roof.
- A 100,000+ gallon tank probably requires more roof catchment unless the owner accepts that it may often not fill.
- Tank foundation/base design is critical because full tanks are extremely heavy.
- Clean roof water should not be mixed with dirty surface runoff.
- Potable use requires treatment, testing, and backflow protection.
- Pump design depends on total dynamic head, not just distance.
- The well must be documented before final design.

The app should include:
- Dashboard
- Intake questions
- Task board
- Calculators
- Tank planning
- Pumping and distribution planning
- Well investigation
- Water treatment
- Risk register
- Contractor bid package
- Document library
- Ranch-scale water planning
- Maintenance plan
