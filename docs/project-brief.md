# Grandparents Ranch Rainwater System Project Brief

Prepared for Luke Peays and family on June 23, 2026.

## Purpose

Build a whole-house rainwater collection, storage, pumping, treatment, and well tie-in system for the grandparents' ranch house. The goal is to supply as much house water as practical from rainwater while preserving and reducing use of the existing Trinity Aquifer well. The well stays as the backup source.

This is not a turnkey-package project. Luke plans to manage the work and hire separate contractors by scope: excavation, tank installation, plumbing, electrical, and water treatment/testing.

## Current Concept

```text
Metal roof and gutters
-> screens at each downspout
-> buried collection manifold around house
-> central roof-washer / first-flush unit
-> 4-inch gravity PVC line to above-ground storage tank
-> metal potable water tank north of house
-> pump and pressure tank near rainwater tank
-> buried pressure return line back toward house
-> exterior manual source-selector valve box tying into existing well line
-> existing treatment path plus carbon filtration and Class A UV
-> house plumbing
-> kitchen sink RO remains
```

The most important design principle is that the rainwater and well systems remain separate pressurized sources that meet only at a controlled, serviceable, labeled source-selector valve box outside the house.

## Known Facts

- Location context: Hamilton / Lampasas region, Central Texas.
- House roof catchment is at least 5,000 square feet.
- Roof material is metal.
- Roof, gutters, and house are roughly 5 years old or less.
- There are about 8 downspouts spread around all sides of the house.
- Downspouts currently discharge onto the ground.
- There are no known gutter overflow issues during hard rains.
- The house is open with no trees within roughly 100 yards.
- House has 5.5 bathrooms and 5 showers/tubs.
- Guest pattern is often 2-5 people on weekends, with roughly 15 guests twice a month.
- Scope is house use only, not irrigation, livestock, or garden demand.
- Existing well is from the Trinity Aquifer and has no known current failure.
- Existing well and pressure tank are about 50 yards west of the house.
- Current treatment includes pressure tank, sediment filter, softener, and kitchen sink RO.
- House is on a slab and the water equipment closet is interior/central.
- Planned tank site is north of the house, about 100 yards away and about 10 feet lower.
- Tank site has room for multiple large tanks, is well-drained, and will be visible from the house.
- Pond is about 50 yards west of the planned tank site and downhill from it.
- Electricity is available at the house but not currently at the tank site.

## Decisions Made

- Design for whole-house potable rainwater with well backup.
- Prefer rainwater as primary when available, with the well as backup.
- Use a manual source-selector valve box instead of full automation.
- Put the pump and pressure tank at or near the rainwater tank.
- Use grid-powered pump operation as the primary plan.
- Treat solar as a future backup upgrade only.
- Tie in outside before the well line enters the house if practical.
- Use an above-ground metal potable tank if quotes and pad requirements work.
- Compare 20,000 and 30,000 gallons as the primary storage options.
- Use compacted gravel/crushed rock pad if the tank manufacturer allows it.
- Collect from all 8 downspouts if practical.
- Route screened overflow to the pond if erosion and backflow risk are addressed.
- Hire separate contractors by scope rather than a turnkey rainwater company.

## Contractor Scopes

- Excavation / dirt work: tank pad, trenching, overflow to pond, expose well line, backfill.
- Tank supplier / installer: quote 20k and 30k metal potable tanks, liner, roof, fittings, vent, overflow, level gauge, pad specs.
- Licensed plumber: pump plumbing, pressure line, source-selector valve box, well tie-in, check/backflow protection, treatment plumbing.
- Licensed electrician: dedicated pump circuit, underground conduit, weatherproof disconnect, pump controls, future backup readiness.
- Water treatment / testing: carbon filter, NSF/ANSI 55 Class A UV, sample taps, water testing, pH/calcite recommendation if needed.

## Bid Comparison Model

Contractor quotes should not be compared on price alone. The working bid score should balance:

- Price closeness to the quote cluster or median.
- Scope completeness and missing exclusions.
- Review quality, including rating, count, recency, and specificity.
- Relevant rural water, trenching, well, tank, potable plumbing, or treatment experience.
- Schedule fit.
- Warranty and post-install support.
- Communication quality and written detail.
- Risk control, including licensing, insurance, backflow, utility separation, erosion, and startup testing.

The lowest bid should only win if the written scope, review evidence, warranty, licensing, and risk controls remain strong.

- Is pH or calcite neutralization needed after water testing?

## Final Working Recommendation

Build a manually controlled, grid-powered, whole-house potable rainwater system that helps preserve the existing well without overcomplicating the house plumbing.

Collect from all 8 downspouts, add downspout screens and central first-flush, run a 4-inch solid PVC gravity line to a 20,000- to 30,000-gallon above-ground metal potable tank, put pump and pressure tank at the tank, return through a 1.5- to 2-inch potable pressure line, tie into the existing well line outside through a labeled manual source-selector valve box, add carbon filtration and NSF/ANSI 55 Class A UV, keep the kitchen sink RO, and test water before drinking and at least annually afterward.
