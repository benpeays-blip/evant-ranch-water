# Grandparents Ranch Rainwater System Project Brief

**Prepared for:** Luke Peays and family  
**Project:** Whole-house rainwater collection, storage, pumping, treatment, and well tie-in  
**Location context:** Hamilton / Lampasas region, Central Texas  
**Prepared date:** June 23, 2026  

---

## 1. Project Purpose

The goal is to build a rainwater harvesting system for the grandparents' ranch house that can supply as much of the house water as practical while preserving and reducing use of the existing Trinity Aquifer well. The system should be designed for potable use, with the well remaining as a backup source.

The preferred approach is not a turnkey package. Luke wants to manage the project and hire separate contractors for each scope: excavation, tank installation, plumbing, electrical, and water treatment/testing.

---

## 2. High-Level System Concept

Recommended system flow:

```text
Metal roof and gutters
→ screens at each downspout
→ buried collection manifold around house
→ central roof washer / first-flush unit
→ 4-inch gravity PVC line to above-ground storage tank
→ metal potable water tank north of house
→ pump and pressure tank near rainwater tank
→ buried pressure return line back toward house
→ exterior manual source-selector valve box tying into existing well line
→ existing house water treatment path
→ added carbon filtration and NSF/ANSI 55 Class A UV
→ house plumbing
→ kitchen sink RO remains for final drinking water
```

The key design principle is that the rainwater system and well system should be treated as two separate pressurized sources that meet only at a controlled, serviceable, labeled source-selector valve box outside the house.

## 4. Major Design Decisions Made So Far

1. **Whole-house rainwater system with well backup**, not drinking-only.
2. **Rainwater primary if possible**, but 50/50 rain/well split is acceptable.
3. **Well remains backup**, especially during drought or maintenance.
4. **Manual source-selector valve box** is preferred over complicated automation.
5. **Pump and pressure tank should be at the rainwater tank**, not at the house.
6. **Grid-powered pump** is preferred over solar as the primary pump power.
7. **Solar can be future backup**, not the core operating system.
8. **Exterior tie-in before the well line reaches the house** is likely best because the house is on slab and closet is interior.
9. **Compacted gravel/crushed rock pad** is preferred over concrete unless tank manufacturer requires concrete.
10. **Above-ground metal tank** is likely preferred for ranch aesthetics.
11. **20,000 gallons is the likely practical target; 30,000 gallons is the best long-term target** if budget/visual impact allow.
12. **Overflow should be routed to the pond** if erosion and backflow risk are addressed.
13. **Hire separate contractors by scope**, not a turnkey rainwater company.

## 5. Recommended System Layout

### Collection Side

```text
8 downspouts around house
→ downspout screens / debris strainers
→ solid PVC perimeter collection piping
→ cleanouts at corners and major bends
→ central first-flush / roof-washer station
→ 4-inch solid PVC gravity pipe
→ tank inlet with calming inlet or screened entry
```

Do not use cheap corrugated drain pipe for the main collection system. It traps debris, is harder to clean, and is not ideal for a potable collection setup.

### Tank Side

```text
4-inch gravity inlet
→ above-ground potable metal storage tank
→ screened vent
→ screened overflow to pond
→ floating intake
→ bottom drain
→ pump suction/outlet
→ booster pump
→ pressure tank
→ low-water cutoff
→ pressure switch/gauge
→ pressurized return line to house
```

### House Tie-In Side

```text
Rainwater pressure line from north
        |
        |-- full-port ball valve -- check/backflow --\
                                                      → common line to house
Well line from west -- full-port ball valve -- check --/
```

This should be built in a serviceable outdoor valve box near the house, before the existing well line enters the slab/house plumbing.

### Treatment Side

```text
Source selector outlet to house
→ existing sediment filter / treatment path
→ existing softener, initially left in line for simplicity
→ added whole-house carbon filtration
→ added final sediment polishing if needed
→ NSF/ANSI 55 Class A UV disinfection
→ house plumbing
→ existing kitchen sink RO remains
```

Rainwater usually does not need softening, but bypassing the softener adds complexity. Start simple, test water, then decide whether a bypass or pH/mineral correction is needed.

## 6. Exact Components to Plan Around

### Collection

- 8 downspout screen/debris filters
- Solid PVC collection pipe around house
- Cleanouts at all major turns and accessible points
- Long-sweep elbows instead of hard 90s
- Central roof-washer / first-flush unit
- 4-inch solid PVC gravity pipe to tank
- Flush/cleanout point before tank

### Storage Tank

- 20,000- to 30,000-gallon above-ground corrugated metal potable tank
- Potable liner, preferably NSF-rated / food-grade
- Metal roof or covered top
- Screened vent
- Screened overflow
- Floating intake
- Bottom drain
- Manway/access hatch
- Level gauge
- 4-inch overflow minimum
- 2-inch outlet/bottom drain as appropriate

### Pad / Site

- Compacted subgrade
- Geotextile fabric
- 6+ inches compacted crushed limestone/gravel, or per tank manufacturer spec
- Pad extending beyond tank footprint
- Drainage away from tank
- Simple fenced utility enclosure
- Small weather cover for pump/electrical/filter components

### Pump / Pressure

- Grid-powered booster pump sized by pump curve
- Likely 1-1.5 HP range, final size by plumber/pump supplier
- 44-86 gallon diaphragm pressure tank; 86 gallon preferred for smoother cycling
- 40/60 pressure switch, final setting should coordinate with existing well pressure
- Low-water cutoff
- Pressure gauge
- Pressure relief valve
- Check valve
- Hose bib / flush port
- Isolation valves and unions

### Return Line

- Potable-rated pressure line from tank to house
- 1.5-inch minimum; 2-inch preferred for lower friction and future margin
- Buried in same general trench corridor as gravity line/electric if code-compliant separation is maintained
- Tracer wire / marking tape recommended
- Isolation valves at both ends

### Tie-In / Source Selector

- Large rectangular valve box, not tiny sprinkler box
- Gravel bottom for drainage
- Rainwater full-port ball valve
- Well full-port ball valve
- Check valve/backflow protection on each source
- Common outlet to house
- Unions for service
- Optional pressure gauge
- Optional flush/hose bib
- Clear labels: RAIN, WELL, TO HOUSE

### Treatment

- Existing sediment filter retained or replaced/upgraded
- Existing softener retained initially
- Whole-house carbon filter added
- Final sediment polishing filter if needed
- NSF/ANSI 55 Class A UV unit sized for whole-house flow
- UV alarm/shutoff preferred if budget allows
- Sample taps before and after treatment
- Kitchen sink RO remains
- Optional calcite/pH neutralizer only if testing shows low pH/corrosivity

Possible supplier categories:

- Corrugated metal potable tank suppliers near Austin/Central Texas

### Trade 3: Licensed Plumber

Primary responsibilities:

- Potable water piping
- Pump/pressure tank plumbing
- Source-selector valve box

- Check valves/backflow protection
- Treatment plumbing
- Sample taps
- Startup flushing/support

Potential local lead from previous research:

- Ashmore Plumbing - Hamilton area
- Other licensed Hamilton/Lampasas plumbers

Important instruction for plumber:

> Build an exterior manual source-selector manifold before the house water entry. Rainwater from the north and well water from the west should each have a full-port shutoff and check/backflow protection before joining the house supply. The systems must not push backward into each other.

### Trade 4: Licensed Electrician

Primary responsibilities:

- Dedicated pump circuit from house to tank site
- Buried conduit
- Weatherproof disconnect
- Power for pump, controls, and possibly level monitoring
- Proper code-compliant separation from water lines
- Future solar/generator backup readiness if desired

Potential leads from previous research:

- Malco Electric
- Other licensed rural/Hill Country electrician

### Trade 5: Water Treatment / Testing Company

Primary responsibilities:

- Size and install whole-house carbon filter
- Size and install NSF/ANSI 55 Class A UV unit
- Evaluate existing softener and sediment filter
- Add sample taps
- Recommend pH/calcite only if testing shows need
- Arrange water testing or direct family to certified lab

Potential categories:

- Local water treatment company familiar with wells and rural homes
- Company that installs UV, carbon, sediment, and RO equipment

## 12. Final Working Recommendation

Build a manually controlled, grid-powered, whole-house potable rainwater system that helps preserve the existing well without overcomplicating the house plumbing.

The recommended build is:

- Collect from all 8 downspouts.
- Add downspout screens and central first-flush.
- Run 4-inch solid PVC gravity line to tank.
- Install 20,000-30,000 gallon above-ground metal potable tank on compacted gravel pad.
- Put pump and pressure tank at the tank.
- Run 1.5-2 inch potable pressure line back toward house.
- Tie into existing well line outside through a labeled manual source-selector valve box.
- Keep well as backup.
- Add carbon filtration and NSF/ANSI 55 Class A UV before household use.
- Keep kitchen sink RO.
- Test water before drinking and at least annually afterward.
